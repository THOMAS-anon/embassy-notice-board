Document Authentication & Legalization

Documents from Ethiopia (for use in China)

  1. Notarization in Ethiopia:
    Get your document notarized by the issuing authority in Ethiopia.
  2. Authentication by Ethiopian MoFA:
    Have the document authenticated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MoFA) in Addis Ababa.
  3. Authentication by Chinese Embassy in Ethiopia:
    Then legalized by the Embassy of the People’s Republic of China in Ethiopia.

    Contacts: chinaemb_et@mfa.gov.cn, chineseembassy@telecom.net.et

  4. Submit at Ethiopian Embassy in Beijing:
    Present the legalized document:

Power of Attorney (PoA) Service

Step 1: Visit the Ethiopian Embassy in Beijing

What You Need to Bring (Documents required):
  • For the person giving Power of Attorney (Grantor): Original passport + one photocopy
  • For the person receiving Power of Attorney (Attorney-in-fact): One copy of their valid identification (e.g., Resident Permit, National ID – Ethiopia, or Driver’s License)

Service Fee: 331.80 RMB (Cash only)

Processing Time: ~15 minutes (upon arrival during working hours)

Service Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM

Step 2: Send the Document to Ethiopia

After receiving the Power of Attorney from the embassy, send the original hard copy to the person receiving the Power of Attorney in Ethiopia.

Step 3: Authenticate at the Ethiopian Ministry of Foreign Affairs – Consular Affairs Directorate General

The recipient must first get the document verified and authenticated by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs – Consular Affairs Directorate General.

Address: 2QF4+8JH, Wendmanah St, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Step 4: Final Authentication at Federal Documents Office – Documents Authentication and Registration Service (DARS)

After Ministry approval, the recipient must visit the Federal Documents Authentication and Registration Service (DARS) for final verification.

Branch Locations: Check the nearest office here → www.dars.gov.et

Important Notes

  • All submitted documents must be original. Any attempt to present forged, plagiarized, or altered documents is strictly prohibited and constitutes a legal offense. Individuals found submitting such documents will be held legally accountable in accordance with applicable laws and regulations.
  • Payment must be in cash (RMB).
  • Processing is done in person during embassy working hours.

Police Clearance & Fingerprint Certification

Step 1: Visit the Ethiopian Embassy in Beijing

What You Need to Bring (Applicant):
  • Original passport + one photocopy
  • Five (5) photos (3cm × 4cm)

Service Fee: Free of charge

Processing Time: ~20 minutes (upon arrival during working hours)

Service Hours: Monday–Friday, 8:30 AM – 5:00 PM

What Happens Next?
  • Your fingerprints will be taken.
  • The officer will sign the document, place it with your photos in a sealed envelope, and stamp the envelope for security.
  • You will receive the sealed envelope — DO NOT OPEN IT.

Step 2: Send the Sealed Document to Ethiopia

Mail the sealed envelope to Ethiopia for further processing.

Step 3: Processing in Ethiopia

Submit the sealed envelope to the Federal Police Criminal Investigation Sector – Forensic Investigation Directorate. Only they are authorized to open the sealed envelope.

Step 4: Authentication at Ethiopian Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MoFA)

After police clearance, have the document verified and stamped by the Ethiopian Ministry of Foreign Affairs – Consular Affairs.

Address: 2QF4+8JH, Wendmanah St, Addis Ababa, Ethiopia

Step 5: Final Verification at the Chinese Embassy in Ethiopia

Legalize the document at the Embassy of the People’s Republic of China in Ethiopia. Check their website or contact emails for exact address and requirements.

Contacts: chinaemb_et@mfa.gov.cn, chineseembassy@telecom.net.et

Important Notes

  • DO NOT OPEN the sealed envelope from the Ethiopian Embassy.
  • All steps must be completed in order.
  • Processing is done in person during embassy working hours.
